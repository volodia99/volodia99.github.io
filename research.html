    • Observational signatures of eccentric Jupiters inside gas cavities in protoplanetary discs

      Baruteau C., Wafflard-Fernandez G., Le Gal R., Debras F., Carmona A., Fuente A., Rivière-Marichalar P.

      An eccentric warm Jupiter inside a cavity generates asymmetries and velocity perturbations in the 12^{12}12CO emission, visible with ALMA, but has little effect on the 13^{13}13CO and C18^{18}18O emission, on the radio continuum of dust thermal emission and on the light scattered by them in near-IR.
